Donate for the injured in Anti-Discrimination Students Movement 2024

In July 2024, Bangladesh experienced a significant student-led movement known as the Anti-Discrimination Students Movement. Initially, the movement sought reforms to the civil service reservation quota system, which many perceived as discriminatory. However, it rapidly evolved into a broader pro-democracy uprising, culminating in the resignation and departure of Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina in August 2024. Unfortunately, many participants were injured during clashes with law enforcement and counter-protesters.
